Santiago Bernabéu Stadium

History

The Santiago Bernabéu Stadium, named after the legendary Real Madrid president Santiago Bernabéu, was inaugurated on December 14, 1947. It has since become one of the most famous and prestigious football stadiums in the world.

Features

  • Capacity: 81,044
  • Location: Madrid, Spain
  • Surface: Grass
  • Owner: Real Madrid CF

Key Events

The Santiago Bernabéu has hosted numerous significant events, including multiple European Cup/Champions League finals, FIFA World Cup matches, and the UEFA Euro 1964 final. It is renowned for its electrifying atmosphere, especially during major matches.

Recent Renovations

In recent years, the stadium has undergone extensive renovations to enhance its facilities and increase its capacity. The modernized Bernabéu features a retractable roof, new seating areas, and advanced technological amenities to improve the fan experience.
